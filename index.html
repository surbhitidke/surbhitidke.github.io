About Me

Background & Research

I'm a PhD candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Central Florida, working in Dr. Swaminathan Rajaraman's NanoBioSensors & Systems Lab.

My research focuses on developing multimodal biosensor platforms that combine TEER electrodes with pH and oxygen sensing for in vitro cellular models and wearable applications. I work across the full fabrication pipeline, from mask design and photolithography to thin film deposition, materials characterization, and electrical testing.

Originally from India, I've worked in Singapore before pursuing my PhD. I've mentored 2 REU students, presented at multiple conferences, and published in peer-reviewed journals.

Looking for: Process Engineer, MEMS Engineer, Hardware Engineer, or R&D Scientist roles in semiconductor, medtech, or aerospace industries.
