About Me

Background & Research

PhD Candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Central Florida, conducting research in Dr. Rajaraman's NanoBioSensors & Systems Lab.

My research spans semiconductor process development and MEMS microfabrication of electrochemical and impedimetric biosensor electrodes on glass and polymer substrates. I execute the full fabrication pipeline: photolithography (Heidelberg DWL, mask aligner), thin film deposition (PVD, CVD, ALD, E-beam evaporation), wet/dry etching, lift-off, and materials characterization (SEM, XPS, AFM, profilometry). I also design multi-layer mixed-signal PCBs in Altium Designer for sensor signal acquisition and hardware validation.

Core competencies: Cleanroom operations (Class 100), Design of Experiments (DOE), statistical process control, yield optimization (40%→95%), and design for manufacturing (DFM).

Target roles: Semiconductor Process Engineer, Thin Film Engineer, MEMS Engineer, Hardware R&D Engineer, or Materials Scientist at semiconductor fabs, medtech companies, national laboratories, or aerospace firms.

Embedded, Multimodal (TEER, EIS, Transparency) Sensing Chips for In Vitro Cellular Models

S. Tidke (First Author), M. Tregansin, J. Potter, C. Hatcher, A. Watson

IEEE Sensors Letters ✓ Peer-Reviewed IEEE Journal · Impact Factor: 2.8

Major Significance

This work addresses a critical bottleneck in the $15B U.S. pharmaceutical drug development pipeline: the inability to test drugs on living human tissue without animal testing. The sensing chip I engineered enables pharmaceutical companies to screen drug candidates on lab-grown human cells, potentially reducing drug development timelines by 2-3 years and saving billions in failed clinical trials. This technology was commercialized through a partnership with Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), demonstrating immediate industry adoption.

MEMS Thin Film Deposition Electrochemical Sensing Industry Commercialization

Development of a Cost-Effective, Hybrid Microfabricated, All-Glass Hermetic Package for Wireless Electrode Array Systems

F.B. Kashem, J.M. Castro, O.C. Torres, S. Tidke, C. McDorman, G. Davies, et al.

IEEE SENSORS Conference 2025 ✓ Premier IEEE Sensors Conference · 2,500+ Attendees

Major Significance

Hermetic packaging is essential for implantable medical devices like pacemakers and neural interfaces — a market projected to exceed $12B by 2028. This research introduces a novel all-glass hermetic package that reduces manufacturing costs by 40% compared to traditional ceramic packages while maintaining the same reliability standards required by the FDA. My contribution focused on the microfabrication process development and thin-film metallization stack (Ti/Pt) optimization.

Hermetic Packaging Cleanroom Processing Cost Reduction Medical Devices
2024

Tailoring Enhancement of Silicon Dioxide Adhesion to Polycarbonate Substrates for 3D Microelectrode Arrays

S. Tidke (First Author), O.S. Cepeda-Torres, P. Morales-Cruz, B.E. Butkus, A. Childs, et al.

Hilton Head Workshop 2024 ✓ Elite MEMS Conference · <30% Acceptance Rate

Major Significance

A fundamental challenge in semiconductor manufacturing is achieving reliable thin-film adhesion on polymer substrates — a problem that causes millions of dollars in yield loss annually. This research systematically solved this problem using silane coupling chemistry, improving first-pass yield from 40% to 95% while maintaining 85% optical transparency. The methodology I developed is directly transferable to flexible electronics, wearable sensors, and next-generation display manufacturing.

Yield Optimization Thin Film Adhesion Process Development Statistical Process Control
2019

Guiding and Controlling Light at Nanoscale in Field Effect Transistor

L. Singh, S. Tidke, M. Kumar

Applied Physics B: Lasers and Optics ✓ Springer Nature Journal · 4 Citations

Major Significance

As transistors approach atomic scales, conventional electronics face fundamental physical limits. This research demonstrates a method to control light at the nanometer scale within transistor structures — a critical enabling technology for photonic integrated circuits and optical computing. These technologies are central to the U.S. semiconductor industry's roadmap for post-Moore's Law computing, with applications in data centers, AI chips, and 6G communications.

Nanophotonics CMOS Integration Optical Computing Next-Gen Semiconductors
2018

Metal-Assisted Slot Waveguide Biochemical Sensor

L. Singh, S. Tidke, M. Kumar

3rd International Conference on Microwave and Photonics (ICMAP) ✓ IEEE Conference Proceedings

Major Significance

Early disease detection saves lives and reduces healthcare costs — a national priority. This research presents a novel biosensor architecture that achieves higher sensitivity than conventional designs by confining light in a nanoscale slot waveguide. The metal-assisted design enables detection of disease biomarkers at concentrations 10x lower than existing commercial sensors, with applications in point-of-care diagnostics and environmental monitoring.

Biosensor Design Photonics Point-of-Care Diagnostics Sensitivity Enhancement
